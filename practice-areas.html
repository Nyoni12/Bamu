Human Rights Law

TBLI Human rights law practice involves the application and enforcement without fear or favour of laws and principles that protect and promote the fundamental rights and freedoms of individuals, communities and the environment. TLI believes in the inherence, inalienability, interdependence and mutual reinforcement of all rights. including economic, social and cultural as well as civil and political rights. TBLI human rights lawyers work, including through public interest and strategic litigating, to ensure that people are treated fairly and with dignity, regardless of their race, gender, religion, nationality, or any other characteristic and consistent with the requirements of international human rights instruments binding on countries targeted through the litigation.

Mediation

TBLI is acutely aware that mediation as an alternative to litigation or legal process is often a systematic cheaper and faster method to resolve conflicts and disagreements. As mediator TBLI helps disagreeing or conflicting parties to explore their underlying interests, clarify issues, and work towards finding a mutually acceptable solution at minimum costs and in a way that usually restores relationships and not create a winner lose/feeling that can be harmful to restoration of constructive relationships.
